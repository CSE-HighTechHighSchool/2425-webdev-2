Basilica di Santa Maria della Salute

The Basilica di Santa Maria della Salute is a stunning example of Baroque architecture in Venice. Located at the junction of the Grand Canal and the Bacino di San Marco, this majestic church was built in gratitude for the city's deliverance from the plague in the 17th century. Its grand dome and intricate façade make it one of Venice's most recognizable landmarks. Inside, visitors can admire exquisite artworks, including masterpieces by Titian and Tintoretto.

Peggy Guggenheim Collection

The Peggy Guggenheim Collection is one of the most important museums of modern art in Italy. Housed in Peggy Guggenheim's former residence on the Grand Canal, the museum showcases an impressive collection of works by artists such as Picasso, Pollock, and Dalí. The spacious rooms and beautiful garden provide a serene environment to appreciate avant-garde art. Visitors can also enjoy special exhibitions, workshops, and guided tours that delve into the life and legacy of Peggy Guggenheim.

La Fenice Opera House

La Fenice is Venice's famed opera house, renowned for its rich history and architectural beauty. First inaugurated in 1792, the opera house has been rebuilt multiple times due to devastating fires. Today, it stands as a symbol of Venice's enduring love for the performing arts. Visitors can take guided tours of the opulent interiors, including the grand auditorium and lavish foyers. La Fenice also hosts world-class opera performances, ballets, and concerts, making it a must-visit for art and culture enthusiasts.

Ca' Rezzonico

Ca' Rezzonico is a splendid Baroque palace on the Grand Canal, now serving as a museum dedicated to 18th-century Venice. The museum houses an impressive collection of paintings, furniture, and decorative arts that offer a glimpse into the opulent lifestyle of Venetian nobility. The ornate rooms, lavish furnishings, and exquisite artwork create an immersive experience, transporting visitors back to the height of Venice's artistic and cultural renaissance.

Scuola Grande di San Rocco

Scuola Grande di San Rocco is renowned for its breathtaking collection of Tintoretto paintings. Often referred to as the "Scuola del Caravaggio," this historic building houses over 300 canvases by the master artist. The elaborate frescoes and vibrant colors depict various biblical scenes, creating a mesmerizing visual experience. The Scuola Grande also serves as a cultural hub, hosting events, exhibitions, and concerts that celebrate Venice's rich artistic heritage.

Accademia Gallery

The Accademia Gallery is Venice's premier art museum, showcasing an extensive collection of Venetian paintings from the 14th to the 18th century. Highlights include masterpieces by artists such as Bellini, Titian, and Veronese. The gallery also features stunning sculptures, decorative arts, and temporary exhibitions that highlight both classical and contemporary art. Situated in the Dorsoduro district, the Accademia offers panoramic views of the Grand Canal from its terrace, enhancing the overall visitor experience.
